    • Dissertation: Optoelectronic properties of stacked CVD-grown MoS2 monolayers towards high-performance 2D material-based photovoltaics.

  • Two-dimensional transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs) like MoS2, WSe2, and NbSe2 exhibit strong light-matter interaction, making them highly efficient for optoelectronic applications such as transistors, memory devices, and especially flexible, ultra-light photovoltaics. Monolayer MoS2, with a thickness of just 0.65 nm, offers high light absorption (up to 10%) and a direct bandgap of 1.85 eV, unlike its bulk counterpart. However, scaling up these materials to large-area, high-quality films remains a key barrier to commercialization. To address this, researchers are focusing on synthesizing large-area TMDCs using methods like chemical vapor deposition (CVD) and optimizing transfer processes to preserve material quality. My work aims to transfer and stack CVD-grown MoS2 monolayers to enhance light absorption while retaining their intrinsic properties, with electrical performance assessed through Hall measurements. The broader objective is to develop scalable, high-performance solar cells using vertical carrier transport architectures, which have been shown to outperform lateral designs in model projections. Following are the key points of my research:
  • 1. Synthesizing monolayer transition-metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs) via chemical vapor deposition (CVD).
  • 2. Characterizing synthesized films through measurements of photoluminescence quantum yield (PLQY), optical absorption, Raman spectra, mobility, film thickness, and more.
  • 3. Fabricating small-area and large-area Schottky photovoltaic (PV) devices using electron beam lithography and E-beam evaporation techniques.
  • 4. Developing and implementing a novel mesh transfer method for transferring monolayer films onto Schottky PV devices.
  • 5. Evaluating the performance of Schottky PV cells by analyzing IV curves, internal quantum efficiency (IQE), external quantum efficiency (EQE), and other metrics.
  • 6. Creating a scalable transfer process to stack large-area CVD-grown MoS2 films, aiming to enhance absorption while preserving the direct bandgap properties of monolayer MoS2.
