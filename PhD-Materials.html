Dissertation:

Optoelectronic Properties of Stacked CVD-grown MoS2 Monolayers for High-Performance 2D Material-Based Photovoltaics

Synthesis of Monolayer TMDCs

  • Monolayer transition-metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs) are synthesized using chemical vapor deposition (CVD).
  • The process is carried out in a tube furnace with precursors, using argon as the carrier gas. Typical growth conditions include a temperature of 750 °C and a pressure of 2.4 torr.

Characterization of Synthesized Films

  • Photoluminescence: Measured with confocal Raman spectroscopy.
  • Optical absorption: Acquired with a UV-Vis/NIR spectrophotometer and compared with theoretical spectra calculated using the transfer matrix method (TMM).
  • Raman spectroscopy: Performed with confocal Raman.
  • Film thickness: Determined using AFM, ellipsometry, and stylus profilometry.
  • Atomic and electronic structure: Investigated via transmission electron microscopy (TEM) with selected area electron diffraction (SAED).
  • Field-effect mobility: Extracted from FETs fabricated by electron beam lithography (EBL) to define source and drain, followed by metal deposition using electron-beam evaporation. Transfer characteristics are recorded with a Keithley source meter, and intrinsic mobility is obtained by correcting for contact resistance using transmission line method (TLM) measurements.
  • Carrier concentration: Assessed using van der Pauw devices fabricated through photolithography.

Scalable Transfer and Stacking of CVD-Grown MoS₂ Monolayers

  • A mesh-assisted transfer technique was developed, employing PMMA as a support polymer and an aluminum mesh to stabilize films during transfer, preserving geometry and preventing folding.
  • This approach enables stacking of monolayer films, leading to enhanced photoluminescence, absorption, and mobility, while maintaining monolayer-like Raman features and a direct bandgap.
  • A patent has been filed for the mesh transfer method.

Fabrication of Schottky Photovoltaic Devices

  • Both small- and large-area Schottky solar cells are fabricated using 2D materials.
  • The design involves interdigitated, back-contact, high-specific-power MoS₂ Schottky devices for space applications.
  • Device performance is characterized under a solar simulator by analyzing IV curves, internal quantum efficiency (IQE), external quantum efficiency (EQE), and related parameters.

Development of Vertical-Architecture High-Specific-Power Photovoltaics

  • A COMSOL model is constructed to simulate vertical carrier transport photovoltaics based on MoS₂/ReS₂ stacks positioned between platinum bottom contacts and ITO top contacts.
  • Device fabrication includes: patterning bottom Pt contacts via EBL and deposition by electron-beam evaporation; stacking MoS₂ and ReS₂ monolayers using the mesh transfer method; and defining/alignment of top contacts (ITO) using EBL followed by sputter deposition.
  • The photovoltaic performance of the resulting devices is then tested under solar simulator conditions.
